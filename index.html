Work

Following my graduation from Wofford College in the spring of 2012, I joined the Systems and Operations group at The National Institute for Computational Sciences. I worked within XSEDE as part of the Technology Investigation Service. In this role, my main focus was the evaluation of software that could potentially enable, or support, research in the HPC scientific community.

I later transitioned into the role of lead systems administrator for The Keeneland Project. During this time, I managed the initial and final deployment clusters.

I became extremely interested with Infiniband networks, eventually joining the HPC Storage Team as a result. In addition to my responsibilities with Keeneland, I now helped manage three Lustre ® filesystems, along with various other storage technologies, totaling upwards of 4 Petabytes in capacity.

Before my departure from NICS, I joined the DANCES project, where I researched Software Defined Networking (SDN).

In the spring of 2015, I joined the Oak Ridge National Laboratory's Leadership Computing Facility as a HPC UNIX/Storage System Administrator. I work mainly within the storage team of the HPC operations group, where we deliver peta-scale storage for the high performance needs of our center. We support our 32 petabyte center-wide Lustre ® file system, Spider, among other storage systems.
I've continued to better my Infiniband abilities, including participating in the Open Fabrics Alliance.
